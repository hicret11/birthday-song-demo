## 2. Reveal mechanics — state of the art vs. our "premiere"

- **Table stakes today:** a branded song page with play button, sender photo + note, shareable link. Universal framing: "watch their face when they press play" — but the product offloads that moment to the giver's phone camera.
- **Only real reveal competitor:** Memorezy's tap-to-unwrap → confetti → play. One tap, one animation. Shallow.
- **Adjacent digital-gift UX proves the pattern converts:** virtual gift-wrap/countdown auto-unwrap at a set time, front-camera reaction capture (Unrapp), scheduled-to-the-date delivery. Sources: [UMA unwrap](https://umatechnology.org/giving-a-digital-gift-give-them-something-to-unwrap/) · [BHN digital gifting](https://blackhawknetwork.com/resources/blog/gc-egc-ungated/all/mar2026/digital-gifting-101-comprehensive-guide-sending-and-receiving-egifts) · [Unrapp](https://www.unrapp.com/).

**Where "premiere" wins:** No competitor stages a multi-beat theatrical build (director framing → curtain/countdown → premiere peak → cast add-ons). The moat is depth of the arc + the "cast" add-ons (AI call, live musician), which no song competitor offers.

## 3. Pricing psychology ( $14.99 / $24.99 + cast )

- **Compromise/decoy effect:** with 3 tiers the middle option wins disproportionately. Two tiers under-monetizes — no anchor pushes people up. [Compromise effect](https://medium.com/@atticusli/the-compromise-effect-why-middle-options-win-in-tier-based-pricing-2a1ff371a0f3) · [Decoy effect](https://growthitect.com/articles/the-decoy-effect).
- **Anchoring:** a visible higher tier makes the target feel smart. The **live musician (~$120)** is a natural high anchor that makes $24.99 look trivial. [Anchoring](https://www.boobook.world/insights/decoding-psychological-pricing-anchoring-principle).
- Emotional/gift purchases tolerate feeling-driven prices; charm pricing ($X.99) still helps at the low end.
- **Context:** AI floor $2–30, human $150–200. We sit mid-AI — win on experience, let cast add-ons carry margin.

## 4. Recommendations (prioritized)

1. **Add a third tier** — make Deluxe ($24.99) the visual "most popular" middle; add a premium "Production" tier (~$39–49: video + AI call or crowd) as the up-anchor.
2. **Always show the ~$120 live-musician add-on on the pricing screen** — it anchors everything below it.
3. **Own the multi-beat premiere in-product** (countdown/curtain → reveal → encore), not a one-tap unwrap.
4. **Scheduled-to-the-birthday delivery + countdown the recipient sees** — proven mechanic no song competitor does well.
5. **Reaction-capture** — front camera records the star's face at reveal, sent back to the director. Category tells givers to do this manually; productizing closes the loop + viral fuel.
6. **Free preview before pay is table-stakes** — make sure the director experiences the premiere build before checkout.
7. **Frame cast add-ons as "casting your production," not upsells.**
8. **Lean the whole funnel on the reveal moment, not the song file** — every competitor admits the press-play moment is what's remembered, yet none stages it. That gap is the wedge.
